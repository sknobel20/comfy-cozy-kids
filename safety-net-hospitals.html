The term itself

A safety-net hospital is one that treats a disproportionate share of low-income, uninsured, or underinsured patients, regardless of their ability to pay. In Boston, hospitals like Boston Medical Center and Franciscan Children's fill that role. They tend to operate on thinner margins and receive far less in private donations and endowment funding than academic medical centers like Boston Children's Hospital, even though they're often the only realistic option for families in their neighborhoods.

Research on hospital community-benefit spending has found that hospitals differ widely in how they prioritize community health needs, and that this isn't just about hospital size or wealth, it's about institutional priorities.

That gap in priorities is compounded when hospitals face financial strain. Reporting on hospital closures during recent respiratory illness surges pointed to how thin margins at lower-resourced hospitals can translate directly into reduced pediatric capacity, right when demand is highest.
